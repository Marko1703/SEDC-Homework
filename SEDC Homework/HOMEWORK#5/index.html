Stocks Surge as Inflation Eases

U.S. stocks rose sharply on Tuesday as investors cheered a moderation in inflation. The S&P 500 gained 2.6%, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average jumped 2.2%. The Nasdaq Composite, which is heavily weighted in technology stocks, rose 3.2%.

Fed Signals Slower Interest Rate Hikes

Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell signaled on Wednesday that the central bank is likely to slow the pace of its interest rate hikes in the coming months. Powell said that the Fed would be "data dependent" in its decision-making, and that it would take into account a range of factors, including inflation, employment, and economic growth.

U.S. Economy Expected to Grow at Modest Pace in 2023

The U.S. economy is expected to grow at a modest pace in 2023, according to a consensus forecast of economists. The median forecast for GDP growth is 2.1%, down from 3.2% in 2022. The slowdown is expected to be due to a number of factors, including rising interest rates, slowing consumer spending, and supply chain disruptions.
